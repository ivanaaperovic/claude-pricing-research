1. Executive Summary

The Landscape

  • The BaaS market is $10.15B (2025), projected to reach $16.75B by 2030 (10.5% CAGR)
  • Supabase leads at ~$70M ARR, $5B valuation, 4M+ developers
  • The standard paid tier price point across competitors is $25/mo
  • Free tiers are shrinking industry-wide (PlanetScale killed theirs, Fly.io effectively killed theirs)
  • Usage-based pricing is the trend but causes "bill anxiety" — predictable pricing is a differentiator

Dotbase's Advantage

Dotbase runs on K3s + Contabo/Hetzner VPS, which gives infrastructure costs that are 10–50x cheaper than AWS/Azure equivalents. This creates massive margin opportunity:

InfrastructureCost to host 50 apps
AWS (minimal)~$150–350/mo
Azure (minimal)~$160–350/mo
Hetzner (2x CX43)~$27/mo
Contabo (2x VPS 30)~$24/mo

If each app pays even $10/mo, that's $500/mo revenue on $24–27/mo infrastructure = ~93% gross margin.

Hyperscaler Verdict

AWS and Azure are not where Dotbase should run its infrastructure at this stage. The costs are 5–10x higher than VPS providers for equivalent compute, and the managed services (RDS, ALB, NAT Gateway) add significant fixed costs that don't make sense until you're at thousands of tenants.
